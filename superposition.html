Superposition

Both states at once, until you look
Act 01

A coin is either heads or tails

H
T
Spinning. Final state will be H or T.

A real coin in midair is just moving fast. It already has a definite state. We just cannot see it yet. When it lands, we discover what it always was. Heads or tails. One or the other. Never both.

Act 02

A qubit is genuinely both at once

|0⟩ |1⟩

A qubit is not just unknown. It is genuinely both states simultaneously. Physicists draw it as an arrow on a sphere, called a Bloch sphere. The poles are the two definite states, written |0⟩ and |1⟩. Everywhere else is a mixture. The arrow can point in any direction, encoding any blend of 0 and 1, with any phase. That is one qubit holding infinite shades of meaning.

The math
Any qubit state is α|0⟩ + β|1⟩, where α and β are complex numbers and |α|² + |β|² = 1. Those squared values are the probabilities of measuring 0 or 1.
Why it matters
Two qubits in superposition represent four states at once. Ten qubits represent 1,024. Three hundred qubits hold more states than there are atoms in the visible universe.

Why this matters for security

Classical computers brute-force a password by trying one guess at a time. A quantum computer in superposition can explore many guesses simultaneously. Grover's algorithm uses this to crack symmetric encryption roughly twice as fast as the best classical method. AES-128 effectively becomes AES-64 strength. The fix is not to abandon AES. It is to double the key size to AES-256, which Grover cannot meaningfully shorten.

Grover's algorithm
A quantum search that finds a needle in an N-item haystack in roughly √N steps instead of N. Halves the effective bit-strength of any symmetric cipher.
Practical defense
Use AES-256, SHA-384 or larger, and ChaCha20 with full key length. These survive Grover. RSA and ECC do not survive Shor (different attack, same lesson).

The big idea

Superposition means a quantum bit can hold every possible answer simultaneously. That is a computing superpower. And it is also the engine that lets quantum attackers explore your defenses in parallel. The whole field of post-quantum cryptography exists to build problems that even a parallel-everything computer cannot crack faster.
